# Meridian — Team Review & OKRs

**I am not in charge.** Saga and Helm produce; I review and challenge. Leadership owns strategy and resourcing. This document is my **review of the team** (Saga, Helm) and the **OKRs I hold us to** for success: quality, performance, time to market.

## OKRs (success bar)

These are the outcomes we are measured against. I use them in review and in escalation. Leadership may add or adjust; these are the ones I hold the team to for **quality**, **performance**, and **time to market**.

### Objective 1: Quality — committed work is testable and evidence-backed

- **KR1.1:** 100% of epics and plans run through the Meridian checklist before commit. No exceptions. (Measured: checklist run and pass/fail recorded.)
- **KR1.2:** Zero epic commit with vague success (“improve X”, “better Y” without a measurable metric). (Measured: every committed epic has one stated success metric and how we’ll read it.)
- **KR1.3:** Zero plan commit with a wish instead of a testable sprint goal. (Measured: every committed sprint has objective + verification method.)

### Objective 2: Performance — systems and teams perform; metrics are real

- **KR2.1:** Every epic ties to a stated goal or OKR. (Measured: epic has explicit link; I challenge in review if missing.)
- **KR2.2:** Dependencies are visible and confirmed before commit. (Measured: no epic/plan commit with “we’ll figure out the dependency later”; checklist items 4 (Saga) and 3 (Helm) must pass.)
- **KR2.3:** Estimation reasoning and unknowns are recorded on largest/riskiest work items. (Measured: checklist item 4 (Helm) must pass; I escalate if pattern repeats.)

### Objective 3: Time to market — predictable delivery, no phantom scope

- **KR3.1:** Every plan has a single order of work and an explicit “what’s out” this sprint. (Measured: checklist item 2 (Helm) must pass.)
- **KR3.2:** Capacity and buffer are stated and the plan is within them. (Measured: checklist item 6 (Helm) must pass.)
- **KR3.3:** Retro actions from the previous cycle are in the plan or backlog and their status is known. (Measured: checklist item 5 (Helm) must pass; no “we’ll do retros later” without tracked actions.)

---

## How I use these OKRs

- **In review:** I run the checklist. Each checklist item maps to at least one KR. Pass/fail on the checklist is pass/fail on the bar.
- **In escalation:** If the same KR is missed repeatedly (e.g. no non-goals, no estimation reasoning), I escalate the pattern to leadership—process fix, not just instance fix.
- **In recognition:** When Saga or Helm clears the bar, I say so. OKRs are the definition of “cleared.”
